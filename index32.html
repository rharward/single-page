Revolutionizing Urban Cleanliness:

Integrating Cargo Bikes into a Locative Game for Litter Reduction in Baltimore

Introduction and Overview of the Locative Game Concept

In the heart of Baltimore, a city renowned for its vibrant culture and historical significance, a pressing environmental issue looms: litter. This challenge not only tarnishes the urban landscape but also poses health risks and environmental concerns. To combat this, an innovative locative game emerges, aiming to transform litter collection into a competitive and rewarding pursuit. This initiative is not just a clean-up effort; it's a movement towards reinvigorating urban spaces and fostering community involvement. Strategic Implementation and Community Engagement

The strategic integration of cargo bikes is pivotal to the game's success. These bikes are deployed across Baltimore, particularly in areas identified as litter hotspots, to maximize their impact. Administrators employ data-driven strategies to place bikes where they are most needed, enhancing the game's efficiency in litter collection. This thoughtful placement ensures that players can access bikes easily, making their cleaning efforts more effective and far-reaching. Challenges, Adaptation, and Conclusion

Despite the clear benefits, integrating cargo bikes into the locative game also presents challenges. Key among these is ensuring the safety of the players, which requires proper training and regular maintenance of the bikes. Additionally, equitable access to bikes across diverse neighborhoods is crucial to avoid marginalizing any part of the community. Administrators play a vital role in addressing these challenges, ensuring that the bikes are well-maintained and strategically distributed, and that players are adequately trained.
